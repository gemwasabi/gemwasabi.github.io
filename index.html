Classification of Attacks

  • Passive Attacks (sniffing, eavesdropping)
  • Active Attacks (DoS, MITM, session hijacking, SQL injection)
  • Close-in Attacks (social engineering: eavesdropping, shoulder surfing, dumpster diving)
  • Insider Attacks (theft of physical devices, planting keyloggers, backdoors, malware)
  • Distribution Attacks (tampering with hardware or software at source or in transit)

Summary of Techniques Used in Information Security Controls

Information Assurance (IA)

Assures that the integrity, availability, confidentiality, and authenticity of information are protected during usage, processing, storage, and transmission.

Continual/Adaptive Security Strategy

  1. Protect
  2. Detect
  3. Respond
  4. Predict

Defense-in-Depth

A security strategy in which several protection layers are placed throughout an information system.

Risk

The degree of uncertainty or expectation that an adverse event may cause damage to the system.

Risk Management

  1. Risk Identification
  2. Risk Assessment
  3. Risk Treatment
  4. Risk Tracking
  5. Risk Review
